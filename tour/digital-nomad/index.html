About the tour

Tbilisi is one of the best cities in the world for remote work

In the last few years, Tbilisi has become a genuine hub for digital nomads and remote workers. The combination of visa-free entry for most nationalities, low cost of living, fast internet, and a warm culture has made it one of the most popular bases in Eastern Europe and the Caucasus. Thousands of nomads choose Tbilisi every year — and most of them spend the first week figuring out the basics while living out of a tourist hotel.

This tour exists to shortcut that process. In 3–4 hours, I'll show you the coworking spaces I actually recommend (not just the ones that come up on Google), the cafes where I know the WiFi won't cut out at 11am, the neighborhoods worth renting in, how to get a SIM card without getting overcharged, and how to open a bank account without the usual runaround.

We'll start at Fabrika — the converted Soviet textile factory that became the city's most interesting creative space. Coworking on one side, container bars on the other, and the kind of energy that makes you want to stay in Tbilisi longer than you planned. Then we'll move through the Vera neighborhood, which is where I'd tell any serious nomad to rent: tree-lined streets, a mix of old Tbilisi architecture and modern cafes, walkable, quiet, with good transport links.

Along the way I'll tell you what I know about the practical side of nomad life here. Where Georgian bureaucracy is surprisingly easy (banking, SIM) and where it's not (long-term visas, residency). Where to eat for under ₾15. Which neighborhoods are loud at night. What the digital nomad community events look like, and how to find them.

I've lived in Tbilisi for over 10 years. I've watched the city change. I know what works for people who want to stay for a month, and what works for people who want to stay for a year. This tour is a conversation as much as it is a walk.
